Mountains

Mountains are a part of the Earth's crust that have been raised due to volcanos, or tectonic plates colliding. Mountains are usually 1,000 feet higher than the surrounding landscape. The tallest mountain on Earth is Mount Everest at a height of 29,035 feet, but the record for the tallest known mountain goes to Olympus Mons on Mars which is at a staggering 69,459 feet.

Most mountain peaks can go up higher than some clouds.
